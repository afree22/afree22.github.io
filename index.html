Research Projects

Health Informatics Research Experience for Undergraduates

Indiana University - Summer 2017

During the summer of 2017 I participated in a research experience for undergraduates, funded by the National Science Foundation, in health informatics at Indiana University. My project focused on designing an electronic toolkit specifically for older adults. I assisted with participatory design workshops and analyzed results from previous sessions to gauge needs and interests of older adults. I created an exemplar object to experiment with the electronics and modify the connections. I assisted with the selection of the electronic components and modified them so that they would be easier for older adults to use. We developed three different types of extensions for the LilyPad Arduino. At the ened of the summer, we tested the toolkit with college-aged students. Although college-aged students were not our target demographic we gathered useful information regarding the usability and difficulties when using the toolkit.

Penn Center for Women's Behavioral Wellness

University of Pennsylvania - Summer 2015 and 2016

During the summers of 2015 and 2016 I was a research assistant at the Penn Center for Women's Behavioral Wellness under the Emily Hartshorne Mudd Summer Research Fellowship. I assisted on multiple protocols concerning female hormones and their effects on health. Daily responsibilities included patient interviews, literature review, data entry, and data analysis. Additionally I pursued an independent research project regarding the inclusion of sex and gender in the research plan of IRB approved protocols at the University of Pennsylvania. I authored a manuscript of the results which has been published in Biology of Sex Differences.
